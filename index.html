The Effect of The COVID-19 Pandemic on Unemployment

Our project studied a dataset consisting of monthly US unemployment statistics for various age groups from 1948 to 2021. We wanted to explore the impact that the COVID-19 pandemic had on unemployment in the United States.

Our hypothesis is that the COVID-19 pandemic had an overall negative effect on the unemployment problem.

Conclusion

Overall, our hypothesis was supported by our data. We found that the onset of the pandemic had an overall negative effect on the unemployment issue in the United States. Overall, the pandemic led to a large increase in unemployment rates, particularly in the early stages of the pandemic. Specifically, unemployment rates for teenagers had an overall spike in April 2020 at 31.7 percent, but has steadily declined since the pandemic.
